## Copay-card and assistance rules

- Enbrel support terms. Commercial insurance only; not valid where paid in whole or in part by Medicare, Medicaid, or other federal/state healthcare program.  
  https://www.enbrel.com/support

- Amgen Safety Net Foundation eligibility. Includes no-insurance pathway and certain qualifying Medicare patients with affordability gap and no alternative support; 2026 income guidance shown.  
  https://www.amgensafetynetfoundation.com/eligibility.html

- Medicare.gov Part D cost basics. 2026 deductible maximum $615; 2026 out-of-pocket threshold $2,100; Medicare Prescription Payment Plan smooths costs but does not lower total cost.  
  https://www.medicare.gov/health-drug-plans/part-d/basics/costs

- Medicare Extra Help. Program to help with Part D premiums, deductibles, coinsurance, and other costs.  
  https://www.medicare.gov/basics/costs/help/drug-costs

## Broader thesis evidence

- AMA prior authorization survey. Prior authorization delays care and can lead to treatment abandonment.  
  https://www.ama-assn.org/system/files/prior-authorization-survey.pdf

- KFF on copay accumulators/maximizers. Explains copay adjustment programs and impact on patient cost sharing.  
  https://www.kff.org/health-costs/copay-adjustment-programs-what-are-they-and-what-do-they-mean-for-consumers/

- GoodRx claim that its prices beat average insurance copays 37% of the time for top drugs. Useful as context, not the main thesis.  
  https://www.goodrx.com/corporate/business/prescription-drugs-cheaper-goodrx-vs-insurance-copays

- HHS OIG report on manufacturer copayment coupons and Part D.  
  https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/all/2014/manufacturer-safeguards-may-not-prevent-copayment-coupon-use-for-part-d-drugs/
